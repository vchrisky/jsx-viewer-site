Privacy Policy — JSX Viewer

Last updated: 11 June 2026

JSX Viewer ("the app") is an offline tool that opens .jsx / .tsx files you choose from your device and renders them as a live preview. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your data.

The short version

JSX Viewer does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. It has no servers, no analytics, no advertising, no accounts, and works entirely offline.

Files you open

When you tap "Open File", Android's system file picker lets you choose a .jsx or .tsx file. The app reads the contents of that file only to compile and render it on your device. File contents are never uploaded or sent anywhere — all processing happens locally inside the app.

Information stored on your device

For convenience, the app keeps a "Recent files" list (file names and paths) so you can re-open files quickly. This list is stored only in the app's local storage on your device, is never transmitted, and can be cleared at any time with the "Clear" button.

Permissions

On older Android versions (Android 12 and below), the app requests read access to storage solely to open the files you explicitly select. On Android 13 and above, no broad storage permission is requested — file access goes through the system file picker.

Network

The app makes no network requests. The JavaScript libraries used to compile and render your components are bundled inside the app, so it functions fully offline.

Children's privacy

The app collects no data from anyone, including children, and is suitable for all ages.
